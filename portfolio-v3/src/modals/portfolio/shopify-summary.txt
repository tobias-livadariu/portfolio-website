Shopify gave me a bigger-system version of the work I already liked:
product surfaces, data correctness, and infrastructure tied together.

I worked on the marketing analytics team, where the code had to support
merchants at real scale. I fixed marketing page crashes affecting about
150 shops per day, helped decouple admin reporting pages from an
expensive legacy data pipeline, and worked through database migration
problems where small test failures pointed to much larger production-data
risks.

The strongest part of the internship was learning how to reason about
safety. A lot of the work was careful and deliberate: reading query
patterns, writing rollout notes, splitting changes into reviewable PRs,
and explaining risks clearly before touching shared systems.

I also built Stickify during hackdays with Waterloo classmates, a React
and Vite tool that turns Slack threads into structured tickets. We pitched
it to Shopify executives, and my mentor estimated it could save about 30
minutes per day for a regular user.
