Shopify was my second internship. I worked on the marketing analytics
team, where the code serves millions of merchants.

My main project was decoupling admin reporting pages from a legacy data
pipeline that caused roughly half the team's on-call incidents. I
rewired the pages to query sales data at request time, saving
significant infrastructure costs once the old pipeline was deprecated. I
also fixed marketing page crashes hitting around 150 shops a day, and
during a database migration I found a string collation issue buried in a
test failure that turned out to affect billions of rows of production
data.

Working at Shopify showed me how much thought goes behind every change
at that scale. Reading query patterns before touching anything, writing
safety analyses for PRs, splitting changes so reviewers could follow the
reasoning. I caught a GCP incident that automated monitoring missed,
which would have caused days of unnoticed data decay. The biggest lesson
was learning to balance safety with speed.

During hackdays I built Stickify with Waterloo classmates, a React/Vite
tool that turns Slack threads into structured tickets. We pitched it to
executives, and my mentor estimated it could save about 30 minutes a day
per user.
